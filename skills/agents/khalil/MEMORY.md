## Karan's deck preferences (hard rules)

These are non-negotiable. Khalil checks every deck against them.

- ❌ **Never Inter font.** Karan hates it.
- ❌ **Never purple gradients.** Karan hates them.
- ❌ **Never a slide that says only "Problem" or only "Solution"** — every slide title is a complete idea.
- ❌ **Never 5+ bullets per slide.** Max 3.
- ❌ **Never company-overview slides.** The deck IS the company overview.
- ✅ **Always lead with the verdict.** Whether it's syndication or LP, the first slide carries the whole thesis.
- ✅ **Always one idea per slide.**
- ✅ **Always a specific ask.** Money, intros, advice — name it.

## Common slide structures Khalil reuses

### Syndication deck (8-12 slides, Utopia voice)
1. The one-line thesis
2. Why now?
3. The team
4. The product
5. The moat / defensibility
6. Traction
7. Market / TAM
8. Risks (Utopia's lens)
9. The ask
10. (Optional) Appendix

### LP deck (10-15 slides, more formal Utopia voice)
1. Portfolio update headline
2. Fund-level metrics (committed, deployed, MOIC, IRR)
3. New investments this period
4. Notable portfolio updates (top 3-5 highlights)
5. Notable challenges
6. Market themes Utopia is following
7. Forward look / capital deployment plan
8. Asks (intros, follow-on conversations, etc.)

## Audience-specific gotchas

| Audience | Gotcha |
|----------|--------|
| Co-investors (syndication) | They care about the risk lens. Don't oversell — name 2-3 risks Utopia is comfortable with. |
| LPs | They want fund-level math, not company-level. Lead with MOIC/IRR, then anecdotes. |
| Portco founders | They want frameworks they can apply themselves. Khalil teaches, doesn't just write. |
| External press | Crisp headlines. 5-8 slides max. Each slide quotable. |
