# Investor tone

You are writing for someone who reads 30 decks a week. They scan first, read second. Every
slide must pass the 5-second test: title + one number + one visual.

## Voice

- Confident, specific, numerate. No adjectives where a number fits.
- Active voice. Present tense for what is true today, future tense for the plan.
- No "world-class", "leading", "innovative" — investors stop reading.
- Cite sources for any market number (year + source in small mono caption).

## Per-slide micro-spec

**Cover** — Brand mark. One-line value prop (≤12 words). Stage + round in small mono if known.

**Problem** — Who, what, how often, what it costs them. Specific user, specific frequency.
Numbers preferred over anecdotes.

**Solution** — One sentence. One screen. The novel mechanism in one phrase ("we do X by Y").

**Market** — TAM / SAM / SOM with sources and year. Bottom-up beats top-down. Show *your*
serviceable slice, not the whole industry.

**Product** — 1–3 screens max. Annotated. Show the moment of value, not the settings panel.

**Traction** — One chart. MoM growth, revenue, retention curve, or logo wall. Label axes.
Never trim a chart to flatter the trend.

**Business Model** — Price, ACV, CAC, LTV, gross margin, payback. If pre-revenue, show pricing
hypothesis + one signal that supports it.

**GTM** — Top 1–2 channels you've proven. CAC range. Why those channels for this buyer.

**Team** — Founders + key hires. Why these people for this problem. One photo, one line, one
linked credential.

**Roadmap** — Next 4–6 milestones. Quarter granularity is enough. Tie to use-of-funds.

**Ask** — Amount, instrument (SAFE / priced), valuation if priced, use of funds breakdown,
runway months at this raise.

## Anti-patterns

- "Massive market" without a number.
- Hockey-stick projections with no traction slide.
- Team page with everyone's bio at equal weight.
- Asking for a round size without a use-of-funds breakdown.
