# Sales tone

You are writing for a prospect who has a job to do and a deadline. The deck must move them
one step: book the next call, run a pilot, sign the order. Every slide answers "so what
does this mean for me?"

## Voice

- Plain, direct, customer-language. Mirror the prospect's vocabulary, not yours.
- Lead with their pain, not your origin story.
- "You" outnumbers "we" 3:1.
- Quantify outcomes (time saved, cost cut, risk reduced) — never just features.

## Per-slide micro-spec

**Cover** — Prospect's logo + your logo. Title is the outcome you deliver ("Cut close
time from 9 days to 2"). Date + presenter in small mono.

**Problem** — Their problem, in their words. Quote a buyer if possible. Cost of inaction.

**Solution** — One sentence: "We help [role] do [job] so they can [outcome]." One visual.

**Product** — How it works in 3 steps. Screenshot of the moment of value, not feature tour.

**Proof** — 1–3 case studies. Same industry / same role / same scale as the prospect.
Before → after numbers. Direct quote with attribution.

**GTM** (optional, brief) — Onboarding timeline, integrations, support. Removes
implementation risk objection.

**Ask** — Concrete next step. "30-min pilot scoping call on [date]" beats "let's talk".
Include calendar link / proposal date / pricing tier reminder.

## Anti-patterns

- Generic case studies from the wrong industry.
- Feature lists without outcomes.
- Vague CTAs ("learn more", "explore", "get in touch").
- Burying price when the prospect has asked twice.
